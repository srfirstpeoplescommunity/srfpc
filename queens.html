The Carib Queens

Honoring the lineage of our matriarchs and community leaders.

Lineage of the Queens


The Carib Queen is a revered figure and matriarchal leader in the Santa Rosa First Peoples Community, playing a crucial role in maintaining traditional customs, heritage, and community cohesion.


Succession of Carib Queens of Arima

  1. Mrs. Jennifer Cassar (Present)
  2. Valentina "Mavis" Medina
  3. Justa Werges
  4. Edith Martinez
  5. Maria Werges
  6. Ma Gopaul
  7. Dolores Medrano
  8. Francis Sorzano

Queen Jennifer Cassar


Mrs. Jennifer Cassar was born on August 4, 1951, at Malabar, Arima to Nicholasa Lara-Pile and Rawie Pile. She is the first of five children. Her ancestral line is deeply rooted in the full Carib bloodline through her maternal great-great-grandparents Jose and Annicasia Lara (nee Lopez), great-grandmother Maria Chichita Lara, and grandfather Pablito Lara (also called Hubert De Franc). Her paternal grandmother was also of Amerindian descent from Guyana.


An Assistant Secretary of the Santa Rosa First Peoples Community and a practicing Roman Catholic, Queen Jennifer has lived the Carib way of life through her maternal grandparents, who were heavily involved in the annual Santa Rosa Festival.


She represented the community at the 3rd Indigenous Leaders Summit of the Americas in Panama City in 2009 and has served on the Cabinet-Appointed Amerindian Project Committee since 2007. Professionally, she joined the Public Service of Trinidad and Tobago in 1971, working across the Ministries of Education, Community Development, Culture, Sports, Health, and the Judiciary.


Her vision as Carib Queen is to fulfill her predecessor Queen Valentina Medina's wish of seeing a united, flourishing indigenous community by encouraging everyone of First Peoples ancestry to return, get involved, and contribute to the community's development.
