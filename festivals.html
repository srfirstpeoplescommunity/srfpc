Festivals & Ceremonies

Living traditions passed down through generations.

Feast of Santa Rosa de Lima


The annual Feast of Santa Rosa de Lima is the oldest continuously celebrated festival in Trinidad and Tobago, taking place every August in Arima. This festival represents a unique synthesis of Roman Catholic devotion and ancient Amerindian customs, celebrating our patron saint, Saint Rose of Lima (first canonized saint of the Americas).


The celebrations feature a grand procession through the streets of Arima, traditional music (including parang, which has strong Spanish-Amerindian roots), and communal dining featuring traditional cassava bread and local dishes. It serves as a focal point for our community, drawing descendants from all over Trinidad and the wider diaspora to reconnect with their roots.

The Sacred Smoke Ceremony


The Smoke Ceremony is a shamanic Amerindian rite practiced by our ancestors to offer gratitude, clean energy, and seek guidance from the Great Spirit and our ancestors. Through the burning of sacred herbs and resins (such as copal and sweetgrass), we send our intentions and prayers upward with the rising smoke.


Today, the ceremony is performed at the opening of major community events, heritage celebrations, and during visits from regional indigenous delegates. It remains a powerful, grounding reminder of our spiritual connection to the land and to the ancestral lineage that preceded us.
